Historical Places in Cape Town

Castle of Goodhope

  • It was built between 1666 and 1679 by the Dutch East India Company.
  • The first stone was laid on 2 January 1666 and it was completed by April 1679
  • The Castle was built by soldiers, volunteers, slaves and Khoi undergoing punishment.

City Hall

  • Built in 1905.
  • Nelson Mandela made his first free speech from a balcony in 1990.
  • In 2017, it was proposed that a full size statue of Nelson Mandela be installed on the balcony

District Meusium

  • Its the Sixth Municipal District of Cape Town in 1867
  • The ‘Hands Off District Six’ conference of 1988 led to the formation of the District Six Museum Foundation in 1989.
  • In 2002 the Museum was able to purchase the building from a generous grant received from a donor, Atlantic Philanthropies.
